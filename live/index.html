The Stats

1 in 5

Women experience perinatal mood disorders (postpartum depression, anxiety, etc.)— a rate that has more than doubled since 2010. Despite these rising numbers, many new mothers lack the support they need.1

12 Weeks

In the US, we don't have a federally mandated paid maternity leave. We also have the shortest average maternity leave among developed nations. This expedited or non-existing timeline leaves mothers having to recover faster than ever.2

~50%

Of postpartum depression cases go undiagnosed. And fewer than 15% of those who are diagnosed receive adequate treatment — a systemic failure at one of the most vulnerable times in a woman's life.3

1 Trost et al., MMWR, CDC (2023); ACOG Committee Opinion No. 757 (2018).   2 OECD Family Database, "Key characteristics of parental leave systems" (2023); U.S. Dept. of Labor, FMLA overview.   3 Ko et al., MMWR, CDC (2017); Wisner et al., JAMA Psychiatry (2013).

Standard Practice

In countries like France, the UK, and Japan, postpartum home visits by healthcare professionals are routine — ensuring mothers receive expert care and monitoring in those critical first weeks. Additionally, cultures across the world honor the first 40 days postpartum with dedicated rest, nourishment, and community support.

In South Korea, over 75% of new mothers spend time in a sanhujoriwon — a dedicated postpartum care center — immediately after birth. For two weeks or more, they receive round-the-clock professional support: lactation consultants, nutrition, recovery care, and newborn education. It's not a luxury. It's just what you do.

Our Process
